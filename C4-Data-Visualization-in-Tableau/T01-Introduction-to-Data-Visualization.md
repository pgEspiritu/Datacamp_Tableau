# 🎨 What is Data Visualization?

**Data visualization** is the graphical representation of information that helps present and communicate complex data to audiences who may not be experts in the subject.

It involves:

- 📈 Selecting the most effective chart for a specific purpose
- 🎯 Applying visual design principles
- 💡 Making complex information easier to understand

Data visualization can even be considered a form of **visual art**, popularized by **David McCandless** through works such as:

- 📖 *Beautiful News*
- 📖 *Information is Beautiful*

Other notable examples of excellent data storytelling can be found in publications like:

- 📰 The Economist
- 📰 The Financial Times

# 👀 Why Do We Need Data Visualization?

In today's world, data is being created and collected at an exponential rate.

Humans naturally process visual information efficiently because our brains are designed to recognize:

- 🔍 Patterns
- 📈 Trends
- 🔄 Relationships

This ability is valuable not only for survival but also in everyday business decision-making.

Benefits of data visualization include:

- ✅ Improves understanding of complex information
- 🧠 Increases information retention
- 🔎 Supports exploratory data analysis (EDA)
- 📊 Enables data-driven decision making

---

# ⚠️ Pitfalls of Data Visualization

Despite its many advantages, data visualization also comes with risks.

As dashboard tools, spreadsheets, and self-service analytics become more common, more people interact with data every day.

Common challenges include:

- ❌ Poor numerical literacy among data consumers
- ⚡ Jumping to conclusions without understanding the underlying data
- 🏢 Lack of business context
- 🎨 Prioritizing attractive visuals over accurate communication

Without sufficient experience or domain knowledge, analysts may unintentionally create beautiful but misleading visualizations.

The goal of this course is to help minimize these risks by promoting best practices in data visualization.
