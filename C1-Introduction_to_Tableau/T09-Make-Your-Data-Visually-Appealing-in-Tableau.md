## 2. The Art of Formatting 🎭

A graph may contain correct data but still fail to communicate its message.

### Common Problems in Poorly Designed Charts:
- ❌ Unclear title
- ❌ Missing legend
- ❌ Small font sizes
- ❌ Missing axis labels
- ❌ Difficult to interpret

Poor formatting makes it harder for users to understand the visualization.

---

## 3. Improving a Visualization ✨

A well-formatted chart is easier to read and interpret.

### Improvements Made:
- 📊 Replaced one line with a bar chart to distinguish a different metric
- 📝 Added a clear and descriptive title
- 🔠 Increased font size
- 📏 Added axis labels
- 🎨 Added a legend explaining colors

### Example Insight:
The chart shows video game sales by release year and region.

📈 Key finding:

> 2008 was the best-selling year, with almost 700 million video game sales worldwide.

## 4. Formatting Tips 💡

Use the following techniques to improve your visualizations.

### 📝 Use Informative Titles
A title should clearly explain:
- What is being shown
- What the audience should understand

### 🎨 Use Effective Colors
Colors can:
- Highlight important information
- Differentiate categories
- Improve readability

### 🔠 Use Readable Font Sizes
Ensure text is large enough to read comfortably.

### 🏷️ Add Legends
Legends help users understand:
- Color meanings
- Categories
- Symbols used in the chart

### 📏 Adjust Axes and Axis Titles
Make sure:
- Axes are properly labeled
- Units are clear
- Scales are appropriate

### 💬 Create Tooltips
Tooltips provide additional information when users hover over chart elements.

Example tooltip information:
- Exact value
- Category name
- Additional metrics

## 6. Formatting Levels in Tableau 🛠️

Formatting can be applied at two levels:

### 📚 Workbook Level
### 📄 Sheet Level

Understanding the difference is important.

---

## 7. Workbook Level 📚

A **Workbook** is the entire Tableau file.

Think of it like:

📗 An entire Excel workbook/file

A workbook can:
- Organize analyses
- Save work
- Share results
- Publish dashboards
- Contain multiple sheets

---

## 8. Sheet Level 📄

A **Sheet** is similar to:

📑 A single Excel worksheet/tab

Sheets appear as tabs at the bottom of the workbook.

Formatting applied at the sheet level affects only that specific sheet.

---

## 9. Types of Sheets in Tableau 📊

Tableau provides three sheet types:

### 1️⃣ Worksheet
Used to create:
- Charts
- Graphs
- Maps
- Visualizations

This is the sheet type used throughout most of the course.

---

### 2️⃣ Dashboard 📋

Combines multiple visualizations into a single view.

Useful for:
- Monitoring KPIs
- Executive reporting
- Interactive analysis

---

### 3️⃣ Story 📖

A sequence of dashboards and visualizations used to tell a narrative.

Useful for:
- Presentations
- Reports
- Guided analysis

---

## 10. Best Practices Checklist ✅

Before sharing a visualization, ask:

- ✅ Does it have a clear title?
- ✅ Are axes labeled?
- ✅ Is the font readable?
- ✅ Are colors meaningful?
- ✅ Is there a legend?
- ✅ Are tooltips helpful?
- ✅ Is the key insight easy to identify?

If the answer is yes, your visualization is likely presentation-ready.
