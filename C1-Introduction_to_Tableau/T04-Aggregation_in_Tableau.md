# 📊 Aggregation in Tableau

---

## 1. Aggregation 📦  
In this section and the following exercises, we will explore **aggregation** in Tableau.

---

## 2. What is Aggregation? 🔢  
Aggregation means **gathering and summarizing data points for analysis**.

### 📌 Examples of aggregation:
- 💰 Adding up total sales  
- 👤 Calculating average user age  
- 🧾 Counting distinct customers  
- 🏆 Finding maximum product sales  

👉 Aggregation depends on the type of analysis you want to perform.

---

## 3. Aggregating Measures 📏  
Measures are most commonly aggregated in Tableau.

### ⚙️ Default behavior:
- ➕ Tableau automatically uses **SUM (sum aggregation)**  

### 📊 Other aggregation options include:
- 📉 Average  
- 🔢 Count  
- 🧮 Percentile  
- 📊 Variance  

👉 These allow deeper statistical analysis depending on your needs.

---

## 4. Aggregating Dimensions 📂  
Although less common, dimensions can also be aggregated.

### 📌 Tableau does NOT auto-aggregate dimensions, but allows:
- 🔽 Minimum  
- 🔼 Maximum  
- 🔢 Count  
- 🧾 Distinct count  

### ⚠️ Important:
When you aggregate a dimension, Tableau converts it into a **temporary measure**.
